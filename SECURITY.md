# Security Policy

Cobalt is a finance application that handles sensitive financial data. We
take security reports seriously and appreciate responsible disclosure.

## Reporting a vulnerability

**Do not open a public GitHub issue for security vulnerabilities.**

Email: **security@cobalt.app** (replace with real address before launch)

Please include:

- A description of the vulnerability and its impact.
- Steps to reproduce, including any required configuration.
- Affected versions / commits, if known.
- Your name and a contact for follow-up (optional — anonymous reports
  accepted).

## What to expect

- **Acknowledgement:** within 2 business days.
- **Initial assessment:** within 5 business days.
- **Fix timeline:** depends on severity. Critical issues affecting hosted
  user data are prioritized over everything else.
- **Coordinated disclosure:** we will work with you on a public disclosure
  timeline once a fix is shipped to the hosted product.

## Scope

In scope:

- The hosted Cobalt product.
- Code in this repository.
- Authentication, authorization, data handling, third-party integrations
  (Plaid, OAuth providers, payment processing).

Out of scope:

- Self-hosted deployments. Self-hosting is unsupported.
- Issues requiring physical access to a user's device.
- Social engineering of staff or users.
- Denial-of-service attacks.

## Safe harbor

Good-faith research conducted under this policy is authorized. We will not
pursue legal action against researchers who:

- Make a good-faith effort to avoid privacy violations, data destruction,
  or service disruption.
- Report findings privately and give us reasonable time to remediate
  before any public disclosure.
- Do not access more data than is necessary to demonstrate the issue.
